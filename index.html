Assistant Professor, School of Information Sciences, University of Tennessee Knoxville

I am an assistant professor in the School of Information Sciences in the College of Communication and Information at The University of Tennessee Knoxville.

I am a co-founder of The MeLa Lab, a multi-university group of researchers that work on problems related to online news and media.

I am a highly-interdisciplinary, computational social scientist interested in using data to answer questions related to saftey in news and media spaces.

Broadly, I view this work from 3 perspectives:

1. Interventions for the consumption of bad information - How to build automated methods for filtering or flagging bad information? What factors impact the effectiveness of interventions? When is it ethical to intervene?

2. Characterizing the production of bad information - Who produces and spreads bad information? What factors lead to propaganda production from governments? What are the dynamics of inauthentic or malicious information behaviors?

3. Measuring the impact of information environments - How does the topical coverage of news impact public opinion? How do different platforms impact information producers and consumers? How do disinformation campaigns work across multiple information environments?

(When I say "bad information" I mean: disinformation, misinformation, propaganda, conspiracy theories, hyperpartisan information, hate speech, anti-social content, etc.)

Both within and outside of this research agenda, I collaborate with many great researchers from many disciplines. I have found that one of the best ways to lifelong learning as a researcher is by learning through diverse collaborations, each adding a different perspective and value to my future work. Because of my many collaborations, I sometimes publish a bit outside of my research agenda. Although, eventually, I always come back to this core focus.

Also, I published under Benjamin D. Horne, but everyone calls me Ben.
