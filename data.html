Publicly Available Datasets

In collaboration with several different research teams, I publish news and social media data for use by researchers. I strive to publish data that is persistently, ethically, and FAIR-ly stored. Below are several groups of these datasets. If you have any questions about these datasets, please feel free to reach out.

NEws LAndscape (NELA) Datasets:


Description: The NELA datasets are large, near-complete news article datasets. The goal of these datasets is to enable large-scale, temporal study of various types of news. This includes mainstream news outlets, alternative, disinformation-peddiling news outlets, U.S. local news outlets, and more. Each dataset comes with rich metadata to enable research out-of-the-box. Our goal is to release at least one new NELA dataset each year. The NELA datasets are a long-term, collaborative effort between the RPI Trust Lab and The MeLa Lab.

Media Landscape (MeLa) Datasets:


Description: The MeLa datasets are large datasets of social media platforms to enable the study of alt-tech ecosystems, deplatforming, and hate speech online. Currently, we specialize in collecting BitChute data. The MeLa datasets are long-term efforts by The MeLa Lab

Dogwood Datasets:


Description: The Dogwood datasets are topically focused Russian-language social media datasets to enable research on Russian propaganda and media influences. Dogwood projects are funded by the Minerva Research Initiative.
